Argajit Sarkar

I am a first-year PhD candidate in the Department of Molecular Biology and Bioinformatics at Tripura University, under the guidance of Dr. Surajit Bhattacharjee, Associate Professor and Head of Department.

Unlocking the power of microbiology and machine learning, my research aims to tackle antimicrobial resistance. It leverages LLM, machine learning prediction, and laboratory validation to drive innovation by bridging the gap between wet-lab techniques and computational methods.

Before this, I had worked at the Laboratory of Molecular Biology and Bioinformatics, where we explored the Combinatorial Antimicrobial and Anti-Virulence Potential of Quercetin with Antibiotics Against Vibrio cholerae. I received my B.Sc in Human Physiology (Sept, 2022) at Holy Cross College, Tripura, India. At Tripura University (Central University), I got an M.Sc in Molecular Biology & Bioinformatics (June, 2024). Apart from that, I'm also an NEP 2020 Ambassador, promoting key policies and initiatives of the University Grants Commission (UGC) and Ministry of Education (Govt. of India).

Research

Biofilm-mediated bioremediation of xenobiotics and heavy metals: a comprehensive review of microbial ecology, molecular mechanisms, and emerging biotechnological applications

Argajit Sarkar, Surajit Bhattacharjee

3 Biotech, 2025

View Paper

This comprehensive review explores the potential of bacterial biofilms in bioremediation, focusing on their ability to degrade and transform a wide array of pollutants, including heavy metals, persistent organic pollutants (POPs), oil spills, pesticides, and emerging contaminants, such as pharmaceuticals and microplastics.

TGF-beta plays dual roles in immunity and pathogenesis in leishmaniasis

Susmita Barik, Sanghamitra Goswami, Prakash Kumar Nanda, Argajit Sarkar, Bhaskar Saha, Arup Sarkar, Surajit Bhattacharjee

Cytokine, 2025

View Paper

Transforming growth factor-beta (TGF-β), displaying a dual role in immunosuppression and pathogenesis, has emerged as a key regulator of anti-leishmanial immune responses. In Leishmania infections, TGF-β drives immune deviation by enhancing regulatory T-cell (T-reg) differentiation and inhibiting macrophage activation, suppressing critical antiparasitic responses.

Harnessing Generative Artificial Intelligence for Periodontitis Prediction: A Machine Learning Approach Integrating Systemic Health Indicators for Precision Oral Health in Resource-Limited Settings

Argajit Sarkar, Espita Ghosh, Surajit Bhattacharjee

SSRN Preprint, 2025

View Paper

Periodontitis affects over 11% of the global population, with severe cases projected to reach 1.5 billion by 2050. In India, 19% of adults suffer from severe periodontitis. This study explores the novel application of Generative Artificial Intelligence (GenAI), specifically ChatGPT-4o, to enhance periodontitis prediction by linking systemic health indicators to oral disease progression.
